WATER POLLUTION

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies, usually as a result of human activities, in such a manner that negatively affects its legitimate uses. Water pollution reduces the ability of the body of water to provide the ecosystem services that it would otherwise provide.

EFFECTS :

  • ACID RAINS
  • DROUGHTS
  • EFFECT OF MARINE SYSTEM

AIR POLLUTION

Air pollution is the presence of substances in the atmosphere that are harmful to the health of humans and other living beings, or cause damage to the climate or to materials. There are many different types of air pollutants, such as gases, particulates, and biological molecules.

EFFECTS :

  • MANY HUMAN DISEASES
  • DAMAGES ANIMALS LIFE SPAN

DEFORESTATION

Deforestation or forest clearance is the removal of a forest or stand of trees from land that is then converted to non-forest use. Deforestation can involve conversion of forest land to farms, ranches, or urban use. The most concentrated deforestation occurs in tropical rainforests.

EFFECTS

  • SHELTERLESS TO ANIMALS
  • DECREASES OXYGEN LEVELS
FOR MORE INFORMATION

MINING

Mining is the extraction of valuable minerals or other geological materials from the Earth, usually from an ore body, lode, vein, seam, reef, or placer deposit. ... Mining in a wider sense includes extraction of any non-renewable resource such as petroleum, natural gas, or even water.

EFFECTS

  • DAMAGES ENVIRONMENT
